# Mad Flask Lab experiment design

Mad Flask Lab uses the loop **Predict → Mix → Observe → Explain**. Each lab card is designed to take about one to three minutes and gives players a safe way to practice scientific thinking.

## Safety and fiction rules

- Ingredients must have playful fictional names, such as Fizz Powder, Sour Drops, Bubble Base, Purple Potion, Glimmer Salt, and Goo Gel.
- Explanations can use real ideas like gas, pressure, pH, density, crystals, temperature, and viscosity.
- The game must not teach hazardous real-world recipes or measurements.
- Every results screen reminds children that real experiments need a trusted grown-up and safe instructions.

## Current experiments

1. **Foamy Fountain** teaches that gas takes up space and can push foam upward.
2. **Rainbow pH Mystery** teaches that an indicator can change color when a mixture becomes sour or basic.
3. **Crystal Jam** teaches that crystals form patterns and that thick liquids slow movement.

## Funny failure pattern

Wrong combinations still produce an outcome. Failures should be visually rewarding and teach observation instead of punishment. Current failure outcomes include cork rockets, soot clouds, slime escapes, and duck portals.
